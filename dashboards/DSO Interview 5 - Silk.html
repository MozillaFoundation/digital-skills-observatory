Learning Interventions

  • Almost half of the respondents that attended the workshop passed the information that they learned on to others.

  • Respondents mainly learned how to use their smartphone to solve real life challenges, to manage their business, and to learn a new language.

Technology & Smartphone

  • The treatment group said their use of technology changed more often than the control group

  • Almost half of the respondents say they don't have any trouble using their phone. The respondents report having a wide range of trouble using their phone, with their phone hanging, network problems and costs being the most common.

Gender questions

  • 20% of the women and 28% of the men say they have something to hide on their phone for their partner

  • Men agree or strongly agree 12% more often with the statement "Men have more opportunities to learn about new products and services than women, especially when it comes to learning about technology"

Digital Financial Services

  • DFS use keeps going up after each interview. For interview 5, 94.5% says they use DFS sometimes, often, or very often.

  • The respondents mainly save to get a loan or to increase their loan limit, and to invest in their business.

Discovering new products and services

  • 40% of the respondents discover new products and services from their friends and family

  • When asked to give their top three of good ways to discover new products, the respondents mention social media, tv ads and friends & family most often.

Insert paragraph here...

Gambling & Betting

  • About 1/3 of the respondents say they bet, but for men this is more than half.

  • 73% of the respondents who bet are motivated to use certain apps and websites by betting. They mention the Sportpesa app, Livescore, and apps that predict the outcome of soccer games.

Lifestyle & Environment

  • Most respondents live in a non-contained house, a house from bricks/stone where the toilet and bathroom are outside. Others live in a Swahili type house or a mud house.

  • Almost half of the respondents say they often struggle when it comes to their living standards, and another 11% is always struggling.
